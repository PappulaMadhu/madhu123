## Problem Statement
Global mortality data is rarely examined across time, age, and geography together. 
This project explores how death rates have shifted from 1970–2010 and identifies 
which demographics and regions carry the highest burden.

## Dataset
- **Source:** Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME) — Global Burden 
  of Disease (GBD)
- **Link:** https://www.healthdata.org/gbd
- **Years:** 1970–2010
- **Key Fields:** Country, Year, Age Group, Death Rate per 100,000, Sex

## Key Visualizations & Insights
- **Mortality Trend Over Time:** Global mortality declined ~50% between 1970–2010, 
  reflecting improvements in public health and medicine
- **Mortality by Age Group:** U-shaped pattern — highest among infants, lowest in 
  childhood/adulthood, rising again among elderly
- **Geographic Map:** High mortality concentrated in Sub-Saharan Africa and South 
  Asia — geographically clustered, not random

## Recommendations
- Strengthen maternal and child healthcare programs in high-mortality regions
- Improve healthcare access in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia

## Limitations
- Data limited to 1970–2010; does not reflect recent trends
- Country averages may hide regional disparities
- Data quality varies across countries
